Dramaturgies of Resistance: Collectivities, Performance, Dialectics

Dramaturgies of Resistance is an online event series running in the 2020–21 academic year. The series is funded by the Jackman Humanities Institute’s Program for the Arts at the University of Toronto and the series is organized by faculty and graduate students in the Departments of Religion, Philosophy, and Cinema Studies. All events will be held on Zoom and are open to the public.

Hito Steyerl, Lecture

On a date to be determined in January 2021, Hito Steyerl will be participating in an event organized by the Dramaturgies of Resistance collective. Steyerl is an internationally renowned artist and scholar who has made a career out of deftly navigating disparate modes of artmaking and theorizing. She is currently a professor of Art and Multimedia at the University of Arts in Berlin. She received her PhD in philosophy from the Academy of Fine Arts in Vienna. Steyerl’s work occupies a singular space in the art world for its animating interventions into the discursive field. The Dramaturgies of Resistance collective, funded by the Jackman Humanities Institute, is committed to bringing to the public events that emphasize the themes of Collectivity, Performance, and Dialectics. We are invigorated and enthusiastic about opening a space to engage with Steyerl’s always relevant and compelling work.
